# Topic Map

This document defines the structured progression of topics within the Aigora curriculum.

The map is organized as a directed graph of prerequisites.

---

## 1. Algebra Pathway

Arithmetic
│
├── Fractions & Rational Numbers
│   ├── Simplification
│   ├── Operations
│   └── Word Problems
│
├── Algebraic Expressions
│   ├── Like terms
│   ├── Expansion
│   └── Factorization
│
├── Linear Equations
│   ├── Single variable
│   └── Systems of equations
│
└── Quadratic Structures
    ├── Factoring quadratics
    ├── Completing the square
    └── Quadratic formula

---

## 2. Functions Pathway

Linear Functions
│
├── Graph interpretation
├── Slope and intercept
└── Real-world modeling
│
Quadratic Functions
│
├── Graph shape analysis
├── Vertex form
├── Roots and discriminant
└── Application problems

---

## 3. Interpretation & Modeling Layer

This layer overlays all domains.

For every problem, the student must:

1. Identify known variables
2. Define unknown variables
3. Establish relationships
4. Construct equations
5. Solve and validate
6. Interpret the result

---

## 4. Mastery Dependencies

A student cannot progress to:

- Quadratic functions without linear fluency
- Systems of equations without algebraic manipulation fluency
- Modeling without equation-solving stability

Dependencies are strict and enforced by the tutoring policy.

---

## 5. Adaptive Flow Example

If a student fails in:

Quadratic Word Problem

The system may regress to:

- Graph interpretation
- Factoring review
- Linear modeling refresh

The progression is dynamic but structured.
