# Curriculum Scope — Fuvest-Oriented Pathway

## 1. Objective

This document defines the initial curriculum scope for Aigora, aligned with the mathematical demands of competitive university entrance exams such as Fuvest.

The goal is not to replicate a full national curriculum, but to design a structured pathway that develops:

- Conceptual understanding
- Procedural fluency
- Problem interpretation skills
- Modeling ability
- Speed and exam efficiency

---

## 2. Pedagogical Philosophy

The curriculum is designed around:

- Mastery-based progression
- Explicit prerequisite relationships
- Concept → example → guided practice → independent practice
- Iterative reinforcement
- Error-aware adaptation

A student does not move forward based solely on completion, but on demonstrated mastery.

---

## 3. Scope Boundaries (MVP)

The MVP focuses on a structured subset of high-impact mathematical domains:

### 3.1 Algebra Foundations
- Arithmetic fluency
- Fractions and rational numbers
- Expressions and simplification
- Linear equations
- Systems of equations
- Factorization and products

### 3.2 Functions and Graphical Reasoning
- Linear functions
- Quadratic functions
- Graph interpretation
- Domain and range
- Transformations
- Functional modeling

### 3.3 Problem Interpretation & Modeling
- Translating word problems into equations
- Identifying variables and constraints
- Multi-step reasoning
- Logical structure analysis

### 3.4 Exam-Oriented Skills
- Time management strategies
- Pattern recognition
- Strategic skipping
- Error detection

---

## 4. Out of Scope (MVP)

The following are not included in the initial version:

- Advanced calculus
- Formal proof training
- University-level abstraction
- Full combinatorics coverage
- Olympiad-style depth

These may be introduced in later phases.

---

## 5. Mastery Levels

Each topic will be evaluated on a 0–5 mastery scale:

| Level | Description |
|--------|------------|
| 0 | No understanding |
| 1 | Recognizes concept |
| 2 | Solves with guidance |
| 3 | Solves independently |
| 4 | Solves efficiently |
| 5 | Applies in novel contexts |

Mastery progression is tracked per topic.

---

## 6. Expansion Plan

Future expansions may include:

- Probability and combinatorics
- Trigonometry
- Analytical geometry
- Limits and introductory calculus
- Advanced problem-solving modules

---

## 7. Design Constraint

Every new topic added must:

- Have defined prerequisites
- Have clear mastery criteria
- Include conceptual + procedural training
- Include error taxonomy mapping
